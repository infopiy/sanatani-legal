1.Definitions

Capitalised terms used in this Policy have the following meanings, consistent with the DPDPA 2023 and SPDI Rules:

Data Fiduciary
The person who alone or in conjunction with others determines the purpose and means of processing personal data (Section 2(i), DPDPA). For Sanatani, this is Infopiy.
Data Principal
The individual to whom the personal data relates (Section 2(j), DPDPA). In this Policy, "you" or "user".
Personal Data
Any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data (Section 2(t), DPDPA).
Processing
A wholly or partly automated operation on personal data, including collection, storage, use, sharing, disclosure, or erasure (Section 2(x), DPDPA).
Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI)
Password, financial information, physical/physiological/mental health, sexual orientation, medical records, biometric information, and related categories as defined in Rule 3 of the SPDI Rules 2011.
Consent Manager
A registered entity, as defined under Section 2(g) of the DPDPA, that enables a Data Principal to give, manage, review, and withdraw consent. Sanatani does not currently use a Consent Manager.
Data Protection Board
The Data Protection Board of India, established under Section 18 of the DPDPA 2023.
Data Processor
A person who processes personal data on behalf of a Data Fiduciary (Section 2(k), DPDPA). For Sanatani, includes Google (Firebase, AdMob), Supabase, and Cloudflare.

4.Personal data we collect

4.1 Data collected automatically

  • Anonymous Firebase User ID — a random string assigned by Google Firebase Authentication upon first launch. It is not linked to your name, phone number, email, or Google account.
  • Device fingerprint — a one-way hash of your device model, Android OS version, and installation ID. Used solely for referral fraud prevention.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token — an opaque push-messaging token required to deliver notifications.
  • Google Advertising ID (GAID) — a device-level identifier managed by Google Play Services. Used by Google AdMob to serve rewarded advertisements. You can reset or opt out from Android Settings → Google → Ads.
  • Crash and diagnostic data — anonymised stack traces, Android version, device model, and app version, transmitted to Firebase Crashlytics for debugging.

4.2 Data you may voluntarily provide

  • First name — optional; stored locally on your device only.
  • Language preference — English / Hinglish / हिंदी / తెలుగు; stored locally.
  • Referral code — optional; transmitted to our server to credit the referrer.

4.3 Data stored only on your device (never transmitted)

  • Daily task completion status (SVADHARMA Dashboard)
  • Current and longest streak count
  • Daily Blessing slots claimed today
  • Ad-free bypass state
  • Notification schedule cache

4.4 Sensitive Personal Data — expressly NOT collected

We do not collect any category of Sensitive Personal Data or Information as defined under Rule 3 of the SPDI Rules 2011, including passwords, financial information (bank details, card numbers, UPI VPA), health data, sexual orientation, medical records, biometric information, or precise geolocation.

12.Data security

We implement and maintain reasonable security practices and procedures as required by Section 43A of the IT Act and Rule 8 of the SPDI Rules, including but not limited to:

  • All network traffic secured with HTTPS/TLS 1.2 or higher;
  • Firebase App Check (Play Integrity attestation) to protect backend endpoints from unauthorised or fraudulent clients;
  • Supabase Row-Level Security (RLS) so that each Data Principal can access only their own records;
  • ProGuard/R8 code obfuscation and resource shrinking on the release build;
  • Screenshot and screen-recording protection (Android FLAG_SECURE) on the wallpaper preview screen;
  • Android EncryptedSharedPreferences for any locally cached credentials;
  • No secrets, API keys, or credentials shipped in the compiled binary;
  • Principle of least privilege for third-party service access;
  • Regular review of third-party dependencies against known vulnerabilities (npm audit + supply-chain audit prior to each release).

These practices meet the requirements of the internationally recognised standard IS/ISO/IEC 27001, as contemplated by Rule 8(2) of the SPDI Rules.
